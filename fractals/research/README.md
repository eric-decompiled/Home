## Key Resources (External)

### Books
- **"Sweet Anticipation"** by David Huron - [Free on Internet Archive](https://archive.org/details/sweetanticipatio0000huro)
- **"Tonal Pitch Space"** by Fred Lerdahl - [Oxford Press](https://global.oup.com/academic/product/tonal-pitch-space-9780195178296)

### Interactive Tools
- [Interactive Tonnetz](https://tonnetz.liamrosenfeld.com/explain-music) - Neo-Riemannian theory visualization
- [Circle of Fifths](https://muted.io/circle-of-fifths/) - Interactive chord/key relationships
- [Sonic Visualiser](https://www.sonicvisualiser.org/) - Audio analysis and annotation

### Video/Inspiration
- Oskar Fischinger films - Search YouTube for "Fischinger Studie" or "Komposition in Blau"
- [Center for Visual Music](https://www.centerforvisualmusic.org/) - Visual music archive

### Creative Coding
- [awesome-creative-coding](https://github.com/terkelg/awesome-creative-coding) - Comprehensive resource list
- [Shadertoy](https://www.shadertoy.com/) - WebGL shader examples

## Research Areas

1. **Cross-Modal Correspondences** - Universal mappings (pitch↔brightness, etc.)
2. **Tonal Tension** - Mathematical frameworks for harmonic distance
3. **Music Expectation** - Why music creates emotional responses (Huron's ITPRA)
4. **Chromesthesia** - Sound→color synesthesia research
5. **Tonnetz/Neo-Riemannian** - Geometric chord visualization
6. **Rhythm & Groove** - Neural basis of beat perception
7. **Visual Music History** - Fischinger, Whitney brothers, Fantasia
8. **VJing** - Real-time live visualization techniques
9. **Generative Art** - Algorithmic approaches
